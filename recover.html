What is Recovery?

  • Definition:

    Resource recovery involves reclaiming valuable materials from waste to prevent them from ending up in landfills. This process not only conserves resources but also supports sustainable waste management practices.

  • Importance:

    Recovery helps reduce the volume of waste, minimizes environmental impact, and can provide alternative resources, such as energy or raw materials. Learn more about Why recovery matters.

  • Types of Recovery:

    Resource recovery encompasses various methods, including energy recovery, material recovery, and organic recovery.

Tips for Recovery:

  • Energy Recovery:

    Waste-to-energy technologies convert waste into usable energy, reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

  • Material Recovery:

    Techniques like shredding and sorting help reclaim materials like metals and plastics from waste.

  • Organic Recovery:

    Composting and anaerobic digestion processes turn organic waste into valuable resources for agriculture.

  • Innovative Technologies:

    Cutting-edge technologies are revolutionizing resource recovery.

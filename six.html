Hazardous Waste Management

Ensuring Safety for Our Environment and Health

Identification

The first step in hazardous waste management is to properly identify hazardous materials. By classifying waste according to its chemical, physical, and toxicological properties, we can prevent accidental exposure and contamination.

Proper Storage

Hazardous waste must be stored in secure, labeled containers to prevent leaks and spills. Proper storage reduces the risk of contamination and ensures safety for both workers and the surrounding environment.

Disposal

Safe disposal methods such as incineration, deep-well injection, and chemical treatment are essential for hazardous waste. These processes prevent hazardous substances from entering the ecosystem and causing long-term harm.

Recycling and Recovery

Some hazardous materials can be recycled or recovered for reuse, reducing the need for new resources. Metals, solvents, and certain chemicals can be purified and recycled in a controlled manner to minimize environmental impact.
